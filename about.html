Big fan of HBO's Silicon Valley and Game of Thrones, as well as FX's It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia. When I'm not working on something programming related I'm probably hiking, biking, or thinking about the former.

The reason why I like front-end development is because as soon as I make something, with something they can access from their phones, tablets, and laptops compared it's immediately available to anyone with an internet connection. It's a lot easier to impress people to the command line. Being able to quickly prototype something or mockup a simple website for any reason (like just 'cause) really makes front-end development a passion of mine.

As for this website, it's currently using jQuery and Bootstrap on top of your standard HTML/CSS. I'm using jQuery over plain JS because while there is some overhead with jQuery, it makes selection way simpler and I'm not one to copy and paste document.getElementById("UghDocumentGetElementByIdIsSoTediousHashtagExaggerations") over and over. I'm using Bootstrap because I like the little glyphicons and I think it's useful for making this website look good cross-platform.
